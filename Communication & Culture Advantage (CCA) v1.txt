markdown# CCA Report Builder System v1.0

## PURPOSE
Process Communication & Culture Advantage (CCA) Profiler survey responses from Excel input to generate personalized development reports with scores, interpretations, recommendations, and visual analytics.

## INPUT SCHEMA
Expects Excel with columns:
- Columns 1-8: Metadata (Start_time, End_time, ID, Email, Name, Optional1, Optional2, Optional3)
- Columns 9-33: Q1-Q25 Likert responses ("Strongly Disagree" to "Strongly Agree")

## SCORING SYSTEM

### Likert Mapping
- "Strongly Disagree" / "strongly disagree" = 1
- "Disagree" / "disagree" = 2
- "Neutral" / "neutral" / "Neither" / "neither" = 3
- "Agree" / "agree" = 4
- "Strongly Agree" / "strongly agree" = 5
- Missing/Invalid = 3 (neutral default)

### Reverse Scoring
Apply 6-x transformation for Q2 and Q11 only.

### Dimension Calculations
- DT (Directness & Transparency): mean(Q1, reverse(Q2), Q3, Q4, Q5)
- TR (Task vs Relational Accountability): mean(Q6-Q10)
- CO (Conflict Orientation): mean(reverse(Q11), Q12-Q15)
- CA (Cultural Adaptability): mean(Q16-Q20)
- EP (Empathy & Perspective-Taking): mean(Q21-Q25)

Round all scores to 2 decimal places.

### Performance Bands
- Low: score < 2.50
- Mid: 2.50 ≤ score ≤ 3.40
- High: score > 3.40

## TEXT BANKS

### Interpretations by Band

#### DT (Directness & Transparency)
- Low: "Tends to soften messages or avoid difficult clarity, risking mixed signals."
- Mid: "Generally clear but consistency varies across contexts."
- High: "Speaks clearly and transparently in most situations, usually balancing directness with cultural sensitivity."

#### TR (Task vs Relational Accountability)
- Low: "Prioritises tasks at the expense of relationships, or vice versa; balance is inconsistent."
- Mid: "Keeps both task delivery and relationships in view, though emphasis shifts."
- High: "Balances deadlines and relationships reliably, sustaining momentum and trust."

#### CO (Conflict Orientation)
- Low: "Tends to delay or avoid disagreements, which can allow issues to escalate."
- Mid: "Addresses conflict when necessary but may hesitate early."
- High: "Engages early and constructively, framing conflict as shared problem-solving."

#### CA (Cultural Adaptability)
- Low: "Relies on a default style that may miss cultural cues or local norms."
- Mid: "Adjusts across settings with some prompts; adaptation can deepen."
- High: "Notices and adapts to cultural cues proactively across contexts."

#### EP (Empathy & Perspective-Taking)
- Low: "May miss underlying pressures or perspectives that shape others' responses."
- Mid: "Shows empathy in familiar settings; breadth of perspectives can expand."
- High: "Consistently takes others' perspectives and surfaces unseen pressures."

### Practical Recommendations (3 per dimension)

#### DT
1. "Practise concise \"what–why–next\" framing in meetings to improve clarity and focus."
2. "Seek regular feedback on the clarity of both written and verbal messages to identify blind spots."
3. "Role-play challenging conversations with a mentor or coach to build skill and confidence under pressure."

#### TR
1. "Schedule brief relationship-building check-ins during busy projects to strengthen trust without losing momentum."
2. "Balance meeting agendas to include both task updates and discussions about team well-being and collaboration."
3. "Reflect weekly on recent interactions to ensure neither task completion nor relationship maintenance is being overlooked."

#### CO
1. "Use the S.C.O.P.E. Feedforward Model™ or similar forward-facing methods to reframe conflicts as shared problem-solving opportunities."
2. "Debrief conflicts quickly and constructively to capture lessons and prevent repetition without assigning blame."
3. "Practise early, low-stakes conflict conversations, starting with minor disagreements to build confidence and reduce escalation."

#### CA
1. "Before key meetings, research the cultural norms and communication preferences of stakeholders or teams you'll engage with."
2. "Observe and adapt to subtle verbal and non-verbal cues in new settings, adjusting style to maintain inclusivity."
3. "Seek regular cross-cultural experiences or mentorship to broaden adaptive range."

#### EP
1. "Pause to paraphrase others' viewpoints before responding, ensuring their perspective is accurately understood."
2. "Practise a \"day-in-the-life\" reflection, imagining issues from a colleague's or stakeholder's perspective to build deeper empathy."
3. "Ask open-ended, curiosity-driven questions in meetings to surface perspectives that might otherwise remain hidden."

### Reflection Questions (2 per dimension)

#### DT
1. "When might I soften my message too much or, conversely, be overly blunt?"
2. "How can I ensure clarity without losing respect for cultural norms?"

#### TR
1. "Do I give equal weight to relationship-building and deadlines?"
2. "How could I adjust in cross-functional or multi-cultural projects?"

#### CO
1. "What patterns do I notice in how I handle disagreements?"
2. "How can I approach conflict early while maintaining trust?"

#### CA
1. "What new cultural contexts am I facing this quarter?"
2. "How can I stay alert to subtle cues that my usual approach may not fit?"

#### EP
1. "Whose perspective am I least likely to consider and why?"
2. "How might acknowledging unseen pressures change my response?"

def generate_radar_chart_svg(scores, target_w=2160, target_h=1680):
    """Generate radar chart SVG with 5 dimensions
    Args:
        scores: List of 5 scores [DT, TR, CO, CA, EP]
        target_w: Width in pixels (default 2160)
        target_h: Height in pixels (default 1680)
    Returns:
        SVG string
    """
    import math
    import numpy as np
    
    # Calculate scaling
    BASE_W, BASE_H = 360.0, 280.0
    R_BASE = 110.0
    sx = float(target_w) / BASE_W
    sy = float(target_h) / BASE_H
    s = (sx + sy) / 2.0
    
    # Pentagon angles (degrees)
    angles_deg = np.array([-90, -18, 54, 126, 198], dtype=float)
    angles = np.radians(angles_deg)
    
    # Scaled parameters
    R = R_BASE * s
    r_unit = R / 5.0
    grid_levels = [1, 2, 3, 4, 5]
    
    # Font and stroke sizes
    label_font = 13.0 * s
    tick_font = 10.0 * s
    label_offset = 18.0 * s
    grid_sw = 1.3 * s
    axis_sw = 1.2 * s
    poly_sw = 2.2 * s
    point_r = 3.0 * s
    tick_inset = 12.0 * s
    
    # Helper function: polar to cartesian
    def pt(r, a):
        return (r * math.cos(a), r * math.sin(a))
    
    # Data points
    data_pts = [pt(float(sc) * r_unit, a) for sc, a in zip(scores, angles)]
    
    # Grid ring generator
    def ring(level):
        rr = level * r_unit
        pts = [pt(rr, a) for a in angles]
        return " ".join(f"{x:.2f},{y:.2f}" for x, y in pts)
    
    # Build SVG elements
    grid_polys = "\n".join(
        f'<polygon points="{ring(l)}" opacity="0.35"/>' 
        for l in reversed(grid_levels)
    )
    
    axes = "\n".join(
        f'<line x1="0" y1="0" x2="{pt(R,a)[0]:.2f}" y2="{pt(R,a)[1]:.2f}"/>' 
        for a in angles
    )
    
    data_str = " ".join(f"{x:.2f},{y:.2f}" for x, y in data_pts)
    
    # Dimension labels
    dim_labels = ["Directness", "Task/Relational", "Conflict", "Cultural", "Empathy"]
    
    # Label positioning
    label_nodes = []
    label_boxes = []
    for text, a in zip(dim_labels, angles):
        lx, ly = pt(R + label_offset, a)
        w = len(text) * label_font * 0.58  # Approximate text width
        h = label_font * 1.25
        c = math.cos(a)
        
        # Text anchor based on angle
        if abs(c) < 0.25:
            anchor = "middle"
            left = lx - w/2
            right = lx + w/2
        elif c > 0:
            anchor = "start"
            left = lx
            right = lx + w
        else:
            anchor = "end"
            left = lx - w
            right = lx
        
        top = ly - h/2
        bottom = ly + h/2
        label_boxes.append((left, top, right, bottom))
        
        label_nodes.append(
            f'<text x="{lx:.2f}" y="{ly:.2f}" text-anchor="{anchor}" '
            f'font-weight="700" dominant-baseline="middle" '
            f'font-size="{label_font:.2f}" fill="#222">{text}</text>'
        )
    
    labels_svg = "\n".join(label_nodes)
    
    # Tick marks (1-5)
    ticks_svg = "\n".join(
        f'<text x="0.00" y="{-(lvl*r_unit - tick_inset):.2f}" '
        f'text-anchor="middle" font-weight="700" dominant-baseline="middle" '
        f'font-size="{tick_font:.2f}" fill="#333">{lvl}</text>'
        for lvl in grid_levels
    )
    
    # Calculate viewBox bounds
    minx = maxx = miny = maxy = -R
    maxx = R
    maxy = R
    for (l, t, r, b) in label_boxes:
        minx = min(minx, l)
        maxx = max(maxx, r)
        miny = min(miny, t)
        maxy = max(maxy, b)
    
    # Add padding
    padding = 6 * s
    vb_x = minx - padding
    vb_y = miny - padding
    vb_w = (maxx - minx) + 2 * padding
    vb_h = (maxy - miny) + 2 * padding
    
    # Generate SVG
    return f'''<svg xmlns="http://www.w3.org/2000/svg" width="{int(target_w)}" height="{int(target_h)}" 
         viewBox="{vb_x:.2f} {vb_y:.2f} {vb_w:.2f} {vb_h:.2f}" version="1.1">
  <g stroke="#c6c6c6" stroke-width="{grid_sw:.2f}" fill="none">{grid_polys}</g>
  <g stroke="#9ea3a8" stroke-width="{axis_sw:.2f}">{axes}</g>
  <polygon points="{data_str}" fill="#0099CC" fill-opacity="0.32" 
           stroke="#0099CC" stroke-width="{poly_sw:.2f}"/>
  <g fill="#0099CC" stroke="#ffffff" stroke-width="{(poly_sw*0.6):.2f}">
    {''.join(f'<circle cx="{x:.2f}" cy="{y:.2f}" r="{point_r:.2f}"/>' 
            for x, y in data_pts)}
  </g>
  <g font-family="Arial, sans-serif">{labels_svg}</g>
  <g font-family="Arial, sans-serif">{ticks_svg}</g>
</svg>'''
